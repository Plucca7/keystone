# Discovery

> The problem this product solves, before the technical spec of how. Fill the sections your work
> level calls for and delete the rest -- see `.claude/rules/discovery-before-spec.md` for the
> depth-by-level guide. This is a worked template: replace the guidance under each heading with the
> real answers. An unfilled discovery on a serious build means no one has decided what is being made.

## Problem

What is broken or missing today, for whom, and what it costs them to live with it. One or two
paragraphs, concrete -- not "users want a better experience".

## Users

Who they are and what they are trying to get done. For a bigger product, break this into personas
(a few named archetypes with their goals and constraints); for a feature, one clear user is enough.

## Value proposition

Why this is worth building: what the user gains, and why they would choose it over doing nothing or
using what exists. State the one thing it does better.

## Success metrics

How you will know it worked -- named, measurable signals (a conversion, a retention, a time saved),
not "it's better". Focus on the few that matter (money, adoption, the critical task), not a dashboard
of vanity numbers.

## Business

Who pays, who uses, the revenue model, what it costs to run, the financial risk, the external
dependency it leans on, and the single assumption that most needs validating before you bet on it.

## UX journey

The main user journey end to end (the happy path), then, for each screen: its empty, loading, error,
and success states; what a user without permission sees; the mobile view; accessibility notes; and
the real interface texts. These feed the feature spec's done-target and the tests.

## Risks and compliance _(regulated / critical work only)_

The risks, the compliance obligations, the stakeholders, what gets audited, and which data is
sensitive and how it is protected. Delete this section for an ordinary project.
